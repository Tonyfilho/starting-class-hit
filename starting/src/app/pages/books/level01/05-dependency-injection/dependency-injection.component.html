Dependency Injection in Angular 19: Managing Services and Providers.

Understanding Dependency Injection

In Angular 19, Dependency Injection (DI) is a design pattern used to manage how components and services depend on one another.

üöö Analogy: A Transport Company

Imagine a customer in Portugal orders a product from China. The customer receives the package at their door without knowing the entire logistics chain ‚Äî which courier picked it up, where it passed through, or how it got shipped. They just receive the package.

This is exactly how DI works in Angular: a component receives the necessary service without needing to know how it was created or where it came from. Angular handles the "logistics" of providing that service.

üí° Why is DI Useful?

  • Promotes loose coupling between classes
  • Improves testability and reusability
  • Makes your app more modular and easier to maintain
